Weeknight Dinner

Garlic Butter Pasta with Whatever's in the Fridge

Melt a generous knob of butter, sauté as much garlic as you dare, and toss with pasta, parmesan, and any leftover veg that needs using up. It's always good.

Serves 2–4 ish
Difficulty Very Easy

Breakfast

Big Pan Shakshuka

Sauté onion and peppers until soft. Pour in canned tomatoes, add cumin and paprika until it smells like a dream, then crack in some eggs and let them set. Serve with crusty bread — lots of it.

Time ~30 min
Serves 3–5 ish
Difficulty Easy
🍗
Sunday Roast

Fall-Off-The-Bone Roast Chicken

Rub a whole chicken with butter, garlic, herbs, salt and pepper. Stuff the cavity with half a lemon and whatever herbs you have. Roast at 400°F until the juices run clear — roughly an hour. Rest before carving. Impossible to mess up.

Time ~1.5 hrs
Serves 4–6
Difficulty Easy
🍲
Cozy Soup

Thick & Hearty Lentil Soup

Sauté onion, carrot, celery. Add a good scoop of red lentils, canned tomatoes, broth, cumin, and a squeeze of lemon at the end. Simmer until thick. Season generously and serve with warm bread.

Time ~40 min
Serves 4–6
Difficulty Easy
🥗
Light & Fresh

Grain Bowl with Lemony Tahini Drizzle

Cook some grains — farro, quinoa, whatever. Pile on roasted veg, chickpeas, maybe some feta. Whisk together tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and enough water to make it pourable. Drizzle recklessly.

Time ~35 min
Serves 2–3
Difficulty Easy

Something Sweet

Brown Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Brown your butter until nutty. Mix in sugar, eggs, vanilla, flour, salt, baking soda. Fold in a big pile of chocolate chips. Chill if you're patient. Bake until golden at the edges but soft in the middle. Don't overbake.

Time ~1 hr
Makes ~18 cookies
Difficulty Easy–Med

Eyeballing Tips

for when you've lost the measuring cups

🤲 A "handful" is roughly ½ cup

For pasta, grains, or greens, one cupped handful is about a half cup. Two hands is a cup.

🫙 A "glug" of oil is ~2 tbsp

A quick pour around the pan — not a tiny dribble, not a flood. You'll get a feel for it fast.

🧂 Salt generously, taste often

Season in layers: pasta water, during cooking, and at the end. Taste before you add more.

🔥 Medium-high is your best friend

Most sautéeing happens at medium-high. If nothing's sizzling, turn it up. If it's smoking, turn it down.
