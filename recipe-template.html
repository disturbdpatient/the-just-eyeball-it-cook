Brown Butter Pasta with Whatever's in the Fridge

This is the recipe I make when I haven't planned anything and I'm already hungry. Brown butter is basically a cheat code — it makes everything taste like you tried. The rest is just vibes and whatever vegetables look sad in the crisper drawer.

Time ~30 min
Serves 2 (or 1 if it's that kind of night)
Difficulty Very easy
Vibe Comfort, no-fuss
🍝 add a photo of the finished dish here

Ingredients

  • pasta — a big handful per person (any shape)
  • butter — a generous knob (more than you think)
  • garlic — a few cloves, smashed or sliced
  • parmesan — a blizzard of it
  • salt + black pepper
  • The "whatever" part
  • a couple handfuls of spinach or kale
  • half a lemon, for squeezing
  • chilli flakes (optional but recommended)
  • leftover roasted veg if you have any

How to make it

1
Get a big pot of water going. Salt it properly — it should taste like the sea, not tears. Cook your pasta until it's just shy of done, then scoop out a mugful of the starchy water before you drain it. Don't skip this.
2
While the pasta's going, melt your butter in a wide pan over medium heat. Keep watching it — it'll foam, then go quiet, then start to smell nutty and turn golden-brown. That's the moment. Pull it off the heat for a second.

👁 Eyeball it

You'll know the butter is ready when it smells like toffee and the milk solids at the bottom have gone amber. Don't go golden — go brown. That's where the flavour lives.

3
Drop in the garlic and let it sizzle for a minute or so. If you've got any vegetables that need cooking (not just wilting), add them here. A bit of chilli too if you're using it.
4
Add the drained pasta straight into the pan. Splash in a good pour of that pasta water — it'll hiss and steam and the whole thing will emulsify into a silky sauce. Toss everything together. Add more water if it looks tight.
5
Off the heat: wilt in any greens, squeeze in the lemon, and snow parmesan over the top. Taste. Season. Eat immediately, directly from the pan if necessary.

Notes & swaps

No parmesan? Pecorino is great. Nutritional yeast if you want it vegan. No lemon? A tiny splash of white wine vinegar at the end does the same job. Add a fried egg on top if you want to make it feel more substantial. Leftovers reheat okay with a tiny splash of water in a pan — not the microwave, the microwave makes pasta sad.
